# Analyze Dirty Reality

Treat the situation as a changing relationship structure, not as a clean equation. Separate observation, inference, metaphor, and unknowns throughout the analysis.

Read [ontology.md](references/ontology.md) before applying the framework. Read [method.md](references/method.md) for the complete protocol. Use [examples.md](references/examples.md) when the distinction between a counterexample, interference, and a hidden variable is unclear.

## Establish the analysis boundary

1. Restate the decision, desired result, time window, and decision owner.
2. Record what is directly observed and what is merely reported or assumed.
3. Identify the cost of a false positive, false negative, delay, and inaction.
4. Mark the parts that cannot yet be known.

Do not start by naming a hidden mechanism. Begin with observable deviation between the expected and actual path.

## Build the structural field

Map:

- actors and groups;
- incentives, fears, obligations, and veto power;
- resources, permissions, dependencies, and information access;
- trust, money, authority, platforms, tools, time, bodies, law, and relationships that materially bend available paths;
- intended action-and-result path;
- known shocks, friction, feedback delays, and maintenance requirements;
- narratives and social signals affecting what actors believe is possible or safe.

Describe “field,” “mass,” “energy,” “entropy,” and “attractor” as modeling metaphors unless the user supplies operational measurements. Never present them as physical laws governing society.

## Explain deviation without making the theory self-sealing

Generate at least two competing structural explanations.

For every proposed interference or hidden variable:

1. Name the observable deviation it is meant to explain.
2. State supporting and contradicting evidence.
3. Assign an evidence level:
   - **Observed**: directly recorded or independently inspectable evidence with source, sample, and time window.
   - **Strongly inferred**: multiple independent signals agree and the causal path is explicit.
   - **Weakly inferred**: one indirect signal or a plausible but unverified causal path.
   - **Unknown**: evidence cannot distinguish the explanation.
4. State what future observation would weaken or reject it.
5. Prefer the simpler explanation when models predict equally well.

Classify an adverse result as:

- **Counterexample** when it contradicts a core prediction under conditions the model claimed should work.
- **Result interference** only when a condition affecting the outcome was defined in advance or has independent evidence and a plausible causal path.
- **Diagnostic interference** when missing, stale, or distorted measurement prevents attribution without itself explaining the outcome.
- **Unknown** when neither judgment is supportable.

Never relabel a failure as interference merely to protect the original theory. Never infer conspiracy, intention, or coordination from unexplained deviation alone.

## Compile understanding into action

Run this sequence:

`定锚 → 建模 → 推演 → 证伪 → 溯源 → 评级 → 验真 → 固化 → 监控`

Produce:

1. the smallest evidence-gathering action that separates competing models;
2. a reversible small test before a large commitment;
3. explicit success, failure, stop, and review conditions; when no baseline exists, label thresholds provisional and first collect a baseline or obtain the decision owner's explicit risk-based threshold;
4. dependencies, single points of failure, backups, permission boundaries, and recovery paths;
5. a monitoring schedule tied to leading indicators rather than feelings alone;
6. a rule for classifying failure as a new interference type, dark-information hypothesis, invalidated assumption, or still unknown.

Use category transfer only when it preserves relationships rather than surface resemblance. State the source domain, mapped objects, mapped relations, broken correspondences, and verification plan.

## Return a structured result

Use this order unless the user requests another format. When evidence is extremely sparse, use a light mode containing only: anchor; facts and unknowns; at least two competing models; smallest discriminating test; stop conditions; uncertainty statement. Expand after evidence arrives.

1. **锚点** — decision, owner, horizon, desired result.
2. **事实与未知** — observations, claims, assumptions, unknowns.
3. **结构场** — actors, relations, incentives, permissions, resources.
4. **现实质量体** — factors that strongly bend available paths.
5. **主意图线与干扰线** — intended trajectory and supported interference.
6. **暗信息假设** — ranked hypotheses with source, sample, time window, independent evidence, causal path, and rejection tests. By default rank with `decision impact × discriminating power ÷ test cost`, or state another ranking rule.
7. **熵增与维护** — decay, feedback delay, correction, boundary maintenance.
8. **事件世界** — narratives, group feedback, time pressure, early rewards, sunk costs, attractors.
9. **竞争模型与反例** — alternatives and falsifiers.
10. **验真计划** — evidence sources and small tests.
11. **行动编译** — next actions, owners, thresholds, monitoring.
12. **冗余与熔断** — backups, stop conditions, exit and recovery.

End with a short uncertainty statement. If evidence is insufficient, recommend a test or a pause rather than a confident story.

## Respect boundaries

- Use the framework for understanding and bounded action, not for manipulating, coercing, or targeting vulnerable people.
- Do not diagnose mental illness or biological states from text.
- Do not treat metaphorical terms as scientific proof.
- Preserve the user's agency and surface material risks.
- When legal, medical, financial, or safety stakes are high, verify current authoritative sources and qualify the result.
